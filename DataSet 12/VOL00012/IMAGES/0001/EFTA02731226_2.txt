VI. <u>Forfeiture/Restitution</u>

We expect to seek restitution in this case. Restitution is mandatory under the Victims of Trafficking and Violence Protection Act of 2000 (the “TVPA”), and includes “the full amount of the victim’s losses” including “any costs incurred by the victim for—(A) medical services relating to physical, psychiatric, or psychological care; (B) physical and occupational therapy or rehabilitation; (C) necessary transportation, temporary housing, and child care expenses; (D) lost income; (E) attorney’s fees, as well as other costs incurred; and (F) any other losses suffered by the victim as a proximate result of the offense.” 18 U.S.C. § 1593(b)(3) (incorporating by reference 18 U.S.C. § 2259(b)(3)). The statutory language is circumscribed by a requirement of a causal connection between the offense of conviction and victim’s harm, but “mathematical precision” is not required.
