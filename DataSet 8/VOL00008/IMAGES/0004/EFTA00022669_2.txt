## Notable Details / Clues
*   **Aircraft Modification:** The "Keystone Helicopter" note alongside the Sikorsky S-76C++ suggests this aircraft was likely converted using a kit from Keystone Airframe (known for HeliFusion conversions) rather than being stock Sikorsky production.
*   **Date of Registration:** The date **8/21/10** indicates this application was filed and signed in August 2010.
*   **Entity Type:** The registration is under a corporate entity (**Freedom Air International, Inc.**), not an individual pilot.
*   **Redaction:** The entire physical address has been blacked out, likely to protect the privacy of the operator or the person who processed the image.
*   **Punch Holes:** There are two distinct circular holes punched through the left side of the document (visible as black circles), indicating it was part of a larger binder or file system before being scanned.
*   **Form Revision:** The footer notes "(1/09)", suggesting this version of the form was in use starting January 2009, supersededing previous editions.
*   **Tracking Number:** The number **EFTA00022671** at the bottom right is not part of the standard FAA form fields and likely represents an internal tracking or archive ID assigned by the organization that holds this document (possibly a legal firm or insurance company).
*   **Signature Repetition:** The applicant (or authorized officer) appears to have signed the form twice in the signature block, possibly because the form is folded or separated into parts (as hinted by the "EACH PART OF THIS APPLICATION..." warning), even though we only see one side.
