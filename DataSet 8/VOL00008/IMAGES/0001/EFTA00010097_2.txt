## Notable Details / Clues
- **Identity Obfuscation:** The subject's name and date of birth are fully redacted, suggesting this document was released under a subpoena or legal process where identity protection is balanced against investigative disclosure.
- **Travel History Span:** The records cover a decade of travel history (2009–2019), indicating the subject was a frequent traveler to international destinations, specifically London and Mexico City, often via Los Angeles.
- **Carrier Evolution:** The list includes "VS" (Virgin America), which ceased operations in 2018. The presence of Virgin America flights from 2017 suggests the report might be aggregating historical data where legacy carrier codes were still used or simply referencing past flights before the airline rebranded/ceased.
- **"NOT ON BOARD" Status:** Two entries (06/26/2016 and 06/05/2016) show a status of "NOT ON BOARD," which could imply missed flights, denied entry, or passengers who did not make it to the destination listed in the columns.
- **Historical Carrier Codes:** The code "PR" appears on flights in 2014 and 2015 (e.g., PR 102). This likely refers to Pan Am World Airways (historically) or potentially Air Transat/Portuguese flag carriers which use similar codes, though less common for direct LAX flights. The code "NZ" corresponds to Norse Atlantic (SeaAir), a known low-cost carrier flying transatlantic routes during that period.
- **APIS System:** All rows list the Type as "APIS," indicating these records are from the Advance Passenger Information System, which is standard for international travel into the US.
- **Site Codes:** "A272" appears frequently as a Site code, corresponding to the Los Angeles International Airport (LAX) immigration office. "A338" and "A273" appear elsewhere.
- **Confidentiality:** The heavy redaction and large "CONFIDENTIAL" stamp highlight the sensitive nature of border encounter data used for law enforcement investigations.
