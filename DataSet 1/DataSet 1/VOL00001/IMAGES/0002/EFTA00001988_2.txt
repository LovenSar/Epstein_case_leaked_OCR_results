*   **Bilingual Title Page:** The presence of both Russian and French text suggests this volume was intended for an international academic audience or a sophisticated collector in St. Petersburg who might be fluent in French (common among the intelligentsia of that era).
*   **Publisher Context:** The text "ЭКСПЕДИЦІЯ ЗАГОТОВ. ГОСУД. БУМАГЪ" translates to the Expedition for State Paper Manufacturing, a state-owned factory that produced high-quality paper often used for official documents and books.
*   **Graphic Design:** The central motif is not an illustration of a specific carpet but a geometric abstraction likely derived from a rug design (knotwork), serving as a decorative header separating the Russian and French sections.
*   **Inventory Sticker:** The sticker on the top left suggests this copy was kept in inventory, possibly by a library, archive, or auction house. The notation "Complete with all plates" implies it is an illustrated volume containing lithographs or engravings of carpets.
*   **Price/Damage Note:** The handwritten note mentioning "£ 1.750" (or similar currency symbol) suggests this specific copy might have been sold, cataloged for sale, or assessed for value in the early 20th century (potentially Russian Roubles or Gold Rubles depending on the currency of the time, though the pound sign is distinct).
*   **Condition:** The paper shows foxing (brown spots) and aging, consistent with items from 1908 that have survived for over a century.
