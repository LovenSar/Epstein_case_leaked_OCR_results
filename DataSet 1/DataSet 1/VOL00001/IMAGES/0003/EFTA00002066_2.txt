## Notable Details / Clues
- **Chronology Discrepancy:** The FBI receipt is dated 8/12/2019, but the warrant shown on the left has a "Date and time issued" of August 14, 2019. This suggests the seizure occurred two days prior to the formal judicial issuance or filing of this specific warrant document, or they are related separate events.
- **Location:** Both documents clearly identify the location as "Little Saint James Island," which is part of the U.S. Virgin Islands.
- **Seized Items:** The list includes a significant amount of electronics (Mac Desktops, HP Towers, iPads) alongside physical media ("Documents, photos, notes"), suggesting a search for digital evidence or financial records.
- **Judge Involvement:** Judge Ruth Miller is explicitly named on the warrant as the recipient of the inventory and the authorizing magistrate.
- **Time Stamp:** A time of "7:20 PM" is handwritten near the bottom right of the FBI receipt, possibly indicating when the items were logged into evidence, though a black-out box obscures part of the "Received From" section below it.
- **Case Number:** The warrant is part of case number "Mag 2019-0060".
- **Form Type:** The right document uses Form FD-597, which is specifically for recording seized property in FBI investigations.
- **Execution Conditions:** The warrant explicitly states it can be executed "at any time... because good cause has been established," indicating a lack of probable cause to limit the search to standard business hours (e.g., avoiding disrupting a school or hospital).
