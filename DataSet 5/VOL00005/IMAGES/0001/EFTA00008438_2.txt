## Notable Details / Clues
*   **Forensic Protocol:** The presence of a "PC Derivative Copy" label indicates that investigators are creating a copy of the data rather than analyzing the original media directly, preserving the integrity of the evidence.
*   **"Clone Copy" Label:** This confirms the bag contains a bit-for-bit forensic image (clone) of the original drive, further emphasizing strict chain-of-custody protocols.
*   **Anti-Static Measures:** The black grid pattern on the inner packaging and the clear outer bag are designed to prevent Electrostatic Discharge, which can damage sensitive electronic components like hard drives during transport.
*   **Specific Hardware Identification:** The label explicitly identifies two different drives: a Western Digital 1TB drive (WD1003FZEX) and a Seagate Barracuda 500GB drive (ST3500). This suggests the device processed contained multiple storage volumes.
*   **Case Jurisdiction:** The text "90A-NY" and "NYC027712" strongly suggests this evidence is related to a New York City police department investigation, likely from Precinct 90A (often associated with Brooklyn).
*   **Date Discrepancy:** The yellow label lists "08/12/2019", while the handwritten note on the tape says "8/16/17". This could indicate a typo in the handwriting, or that the evidence was re-packaged/cataloged years later.
*   **Serial Numbers:** Multiple serial numbers are visible (S/N WCC3F..., S/N 9QM8MT6D), providing unique identifiers for tracking the specific physical assets in a database.
*   **Box Branding:** The box is manufactured by "SMEAD," a common supplier of office and shipping supplies, suggesting standard-issue packaging rather than specialized forensic evidence containers (though the bag itself is specialized).
*   **Cable Presence:** A black wire is visible on the right, implying that power or data cables were present during seizure but may have been disconnected or placed to the side before sealing.
*   **Tapirau Reference:** The label mentions "collected via Tapirau," which likely refers to a specific tool, kit, or protocol name used by the collecting officer, though the text is partially obscured.
*   **Lighting and Reflection:** The strong reflection on the plastic bag suggests bright overhead fluorescent lighting typical of a police station evidence room or processing lab.
